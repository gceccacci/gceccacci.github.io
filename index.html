  My name is Gianluca Ceccacci. I am 19 years old, from Windsor, ON, currently living in Toronto, ON. Here, I am pursuing my undergraduate degree at the University of Toronto in Engineering Sciences, in my second year of study. Beyond engineering, sciences, and mathematics, I am particularly passionate about visual arts, music, and the integration of these factors that have guided my life.
  As a kid, I always liked trying new things and challenging myself with fun hobbies. This includes a love for food, built up during my youth, especially pizza. My younger self was constantly obsessed with technical perfection, especially seen in my participation as a violinist for the Windsor Symphony Youth Orchestra and my constant drive to perfect technical skills in my visual arts. This desire for perfection also carried over to academics and academic extracurriculars such as robotics, where my team was able to win second place in a provincial level lego robotics competition in the seventh grade. Although this is an insignificant accomplishment, it was a pretty formative moment in my appreciation for engineering, and my decision to come to UofT's Engineering Science program, and ultimately pursue growth as an engineer. It was one of the first moments I ever recall witnessing a rewarding product from hard work.
  By high school, I became obsessed with refining my creative visions. During the pandemic, I started creating a lot of visual art -- more than ever before. I always enjoyed drawing throughout grade school, but this enjoyment flourished into a love once I was able to dedicate a lot of time to the self expression it provided. I began studying artists' works and various animated films. I really tried to push my boundaries from technical illustrations to traditional processes to extremely personal subject matter based sculptures. Outside of my hobbies, I got a job at a local pizzeria. After a couple promotions, I was finally at the level to make the pizza I always enjoyed and always will enjoy. Spending around 25-30 hours a week at this establishment, the community and food itself became a big part of my life. I was able to build my social skills here with customer service, as well as learn the importance of the employer-employee relationship. I always made an effort to incorporate my personality and interests into the job, such as showing younger customers magic tricks.
  Now in university, I am focused on combining both the the technical and the creative in engineering, a profession that permits for that so beautifully. With my artistic background, design has always been valued highly. Conversely, I have always noticed strong elements of engineering in my art, such as iterative processes in my workflow or the scientific reasoning as to why some art pieces work. Additionally, I am able to relay information and make use of my customer service skills sharpened at the pizzeria. Overall, it is my ultimate goal to use engineering designas an outlet of self expression, similar to visual arts, in order to benefit others.

Projects

Allan Gardens Vermicomposting System

I worked with local community garden, Allan Gardens, to contribute to the development of an improved vermicomposting system in a team with three other students. This is part of the Praxis II project, where my team formulated a number of designs based on stakeholder feedback, created low fidelity prototypes, and converged to a single solution to present at a design showcase. Based on the input of staff and volunteers at Allan Gardens, we designed a further optimized system to collect worm castings and vermiwash with greater ease, making use of materials already owned by the gardens in order to allign with their goals of sustainability.

Skills: Fusion, teamwork, communication, working with stakeholders, engineering design process





CANSAT Team Member

I am currently working on the CANSAT division of RSX (Robotics for Space Exploration) design team at UofT. Myself and a small team of 9 others are working towards launching a payload ~750 m high in a small scale sounding rocket that will collect varied types of data on it's descent. Although I am relatively new to the team, it has pushed my boundaries in many ways, introducing me to working with microcontrollers, CFD, and PCB design. Additionally, the project has refined my skills in CAD as well as artistic 3D models in Blender. At this point in time, we are writing a deliverable that will hopefully lead to our participation in the June CANSAT Competition in Virginia. Stay tuned for more details.

Skills: Blender, OpenRocket, Fusion, Autodesk CFD, KiCad, Arduino, teamwork, proxy testing
